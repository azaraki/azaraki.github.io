Research

My research focuses on developing intelligent robotic systems that can naturally interact with humans. I work at the intersection of artificial intelligence, robotics, and human-computer interaction to create systems that are both technically sophisticated and socially aware.

Research Interests

Human-Robot Interaction (HRI)

Developing socially intelligent robots that can understand and respond to human emotions, gestures, and social cues through advanced perception and interaction systems. My work focuses on creating robots that can engage in meaningful social interactions, particularly in therapeutic and educational contexts.

Key Projects:

  • Social gaze control systems for humanoid robots
  • Emotion recognition and expression in robotic systems
  • Context-aware social behavior adaptation
  • Multi-modal human-robot communication

Bio-Inspired Attention Systems

Creating attention mechanisms for robots inspired by human visual attention, enabling more natural gaze control and social interaction behaviors. This research combines insights from neuroscience, psychology, and computer vision to develop attention models that can guide robotic behavior in complex social environments.

Research Areas:

  • Visual attention models for social robotics
  • Context-dependent attention mechanisms
  • Real-time gaze control systems
  • Attention-driven behavior selection

Reinforcement Learning for Robotics

Applying reinforcement learning algorithms to enable robots to learn complex behaviors and adapt to new environments through interaction and experience. My research focuses on developing RL approaches that are sample-efficient and can work in real-world scenarios with humans.

Current Work:

  • Actor-critic learning for robotic control
  • Safe reinforcement learning in human-robot environments
  • Transfer learning for robotic skills
  • Learning from human demonstration and feedback

Assistive Robotics for Children with Autism

Developing robotic systems to assist children with autism spectrum disorders in developing social skills, communication abilities, and learning capabilities. This research involves close collaboration with psychologists, therapists, and educators to create effective therapeutic interventions.

Applications:

  • Social skills training through robot-mediated interventions
  • Visual perspective-taking skill development
  • Communication enhancement through structured interactions
  • Personalized learning experiences with adaptive robots

Large Language Models in HRI

Integrating large language models with robotic systems to enable more natural and context-aware human-robot communication and interaction. This emerging area combines advances in natural language processing with robotics to create more intelligent and conversational robots.

Focus Areas:

  • Personalized LLM systems for individual users
  • Context-aware dialogue management
  • Multimodal integration of speech, vision, and robotics
  • Ethical considerations in AI-powered social robots

Cognitive Robotics and AI

Developing cognitive architectures that enable robots to reason, learn, and adapt in complex environments. This research combines insights from artificial intelligence, cognitive science, and robotics to create more intelligent and autonomous systems.

Research Directions:

  • Cognitive architectures for social robots
  • Explainable AI in robotic decision-making
  • Trust and transparency in human-robot teams
  • Continual learning and adaptation

Research Methodology

My research approach combines theoretical foundations with practical applications, emphasizing:

  • Interdisciplinary Collaboration: Working with psychologists, therapists, educators, and domain experts to ensure research relevance and impact
  • Human-Centered Design: Placing human needs and capabilities at the center of robotic system design
  • Empirical Validation: Conducting rigorous user studies and experiments to validate research hypotheses
  • Ethical Considerations: Ensuring responsible development and deployment of AI and robotic systems
  • Open Science: Promoting reproducibility and knowledge sharing through open-source software and datasets
