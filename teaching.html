Teaching Philosophy

My teaching philosophy is grounded in the belief that effective learning occurs when students are actively engaged and can connect theoretical concepts to real-world applications. I strive to create an inclusive and supportive learning environment that encourages critical thinking, creativity, and collaboration.

Hands-On Learning

I believe in learning by doing. My courses emphasize practical programming assignments, laboratory work, and project-based learning that allows students to apply theoretical knowledge to solve real problems.

Critical Thinking

I encourage students to question assumptions, analyze problems from multiple perspectives, and develop their own solutions rather than simply memorizing facts or procedures.

Collaborative Learning

Group projects and peer learning activities help students develop teamwork skills while learning from each other's diverse perspectives and experiences.

Lifelong Learning

I aim to instill in students the skills and curiosity needed for continuous learning in the rapidly evolving fields of AI and robotics.

Teaching Innovations

Interactive Robotics Labs

Developed hands-on laboratory sessions where students program real robots to perform complex tasks, bridging the gap between theory and practice in robotics education.

AI Ethics Integration

Incorporated discussions on AI ethics and societal implications throughout technical courses, helping students understand the broader context of their work.

Industry Collaboration

Established partnerships with local technology companies to provide students with real-world project experiences and industry mentorship opportunities.

Multimedia Learning Materials

Created interactive video tutorials, simulation environments, and online resources to support diverse learning styles and enable flexible, self-paced learning.
