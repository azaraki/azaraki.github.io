I am a Senior Lecturer in Robotics and AI at the University of Hertfordshire, with extensive experience in artificial intelligence, robotics, and human-robot interaction. My research focuses on developing intelligent systems that can adapt and interact naturally with humans, particularly in the areas of bio-inspired attention systems, reinforcement learning for robotic control, and assistive technologies for children with autism.

Research Interests

  • Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning
  • Human-Robot Interaction (HRI)
  • Bio-inspired Attention Systems

  • Assistive Robotics for Children with Autism
  • Computer Vision and Cognitive Systems
  • Large Language Models in Robotics
  • Social Robotics and Therapeutic Applications

Current Research Focus

My current research focuses on developing intelligent robotic systems that can naturally interact with humans, particularly in therapeutic and educational contexts. I am working on bio-inspired attention mechanisms for social robots, reinforcement learning approaches for adaptive robotic behavior, and the integration of large language models in human-robot interaction scenarios.
